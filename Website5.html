Fun Facts

They are diamond-shaped and filled with concentric circles (like bullseye) that make this an easy species to identify. This animal is listed as endangered.

Enviroment

Diamondback terrapins have been observed living in salt marshes, wetlands, mangrove swamps, estuaries, lagoons and tidal creeks.

Diet

Diamondback terrapins will munch down on molluscs, crustaceans, fish, carrion and invertebrates. They use their strong, bony jaws to crush the shells of their prey.

Pet Care

In Florida you are leggally allowed to keep up to 2 of these as pets in your house. they require an aquarium that is large enough for them to swim and grow in size. .
